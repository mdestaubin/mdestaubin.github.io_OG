Master of Design Studies in Risk & Resilience Thesis Project. An agent-based simulation model to test infectious disease outbreak response capacities and strategies.

West Africa Healthcare Facilitiy Assessment

The Role of the Designer in the Context of Social Agency

Ebola International Aid Flow

Data visualization of the flow of international humanitarian aid money to West Africa during the Ebola crisis. The dataset comes from the financial tracking service of the UN.

Portal Kinection

The 2-way portal connects two different parts of a city, or world. It goes beyond the typical 2D world of screen projections with a sense of 3D interaction due to layering and head tracking by a Kinect.

Michael

Born and raised in the southeast United States, I received a Bachelor of Architecture degree from Auburn University in 2012. I then worked professionally at an architecture firm for four years in Boston where I had the opportunity to work on multiple humanitarian and public health projects in developing countries. In 2016, I returned to school to pursue a masters degree at Harvard University Graduate School of Design. Throughout my education and career, I have always held a strong passion in designing for the well-being of others. At graduate school, I've focused on developing my interests in the emerging relationships between design, technology, public health, and humanitarian crisis response.
