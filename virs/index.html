What is it?

An agent-based geosimulation model built to visually analyze the impact of crisis response and intervention strategies

Our prototype model is built to study response and intervention strategies for Ebola outbreaks in West Africa and the Democratic Republic of the Congo. The tool allows users to test and optimize their response strategies through the implementation of both physical and behavioral interventions. The goal is to simplify the modeling process and better equip responders and community leaders with more accurate and easier to understand crisis response strategies.

Agent Based Model (ABM)

Modeling geospatial & behavioral data

ViRS is an ABM that simulates the movements and interactions between a population of agents in their environment across space and time. The model is built to visualize the spatial and temporal nature of an epidemic within the population and predict the level of impact of interventions and response measures.
